Get To Know The Types of Cereals And Their Uses

Published on Friday, 10 October 2014 16:02 Category: Food, Healthy Living

Right from the origin of agriculture, Cereals or grains have played a significant role to mankind. This belongs to the monocot families ‘Poaceae or Gramineae’ which are cultivated widely to obtain the edible components of their fruit seeds. Botanically, a type of fruit called ‘caryopsis’ composed of the endosperm, germ, and bran. Cereal when grown in vast quantities supply more food energy than any other type of crop; they are also known as staple crops.

Cereals are enriched with a lot of vitamins and minerals; it contains iron, thiamine, niacin and riboflavin. Therefore, most cereals have a huge amount of fiber contents, and examples include Wheat, Oats, Barley, etc. They are abundant in Carbohydrate, which provides us with energy. While consuming cereals, it can also lead to a large intake of protein, which we often take in as milk as our breakfast.

There are a lot of cereals which some we are familiar with, while some we are not even familiar with them, but all falls into the category of cereals or grains. We will be discussing some of the widely used cereal and few one’s we are not so familiar with. They include rice, brown rice, maize, wheat, barley, sorghum, millet, oats, rye, buckwheat, etc.

Rice: Rice is possibly the most familiar, cultivated kind of cereal in the world. Though consumed in all parts of the world; rice cannot grow especially in cold regions. Most countries use it as their main food and some restaurants have invented some interesting recipes that go along with it.

Wheat: Wheat is one of the dominant cereals consumed. Commonly in Australia, North America, Europe and New Zealand. Wheat is a significant ingredient in our foods especially in Bread, biscuits, pastries, porridge, cakes, crackers, pancakes, pies, cookies, rolls, muffins, doughnuts, and also some breakfast cereals. It rich in nutrients and has a lot of health benefits in humans body. It is also in a different form and has different species too.

Brown Rice: Brown rice is another delicious of rice that contains a lot of nutrients vitamin B group, particularly riboflavin, thiamine, niacin and minerals like potassium, iron, phosphorous, and magnesium.

Maize: This is a vital cereal in continents like Africa, South America, and it is also used as animal feed all over the world. We all know ‘Cornflakes’ are nothing but the flaked form of maize. A well-known corn product which is called Popcorn has been one of the best snacks for ages all over the world.

Oats: Oats are well known as breakfast in almost all the countries. It’s contains a lot of fiber property. They are vital for all ages and also used as livestock feed.

Barley: This is a (not so popular especially in Africa) cereal, and it’s a highly nutritional one. It’s usually grown for malting and livestock also profits on it where lands are not capable of growing wheat; the cause may be due to financial or climatic conditions.

Sorghum: This is also consumed more in Africa and Asia. It also serves as a good livestock feed. Sorghum can grow in an environment where other crops do not grow well.

Millet: This is widely grown in Africa and Asia Countries. Countries like Russia, China and Germany used it to prepare porridge. Usually used for alcoholic beverages and also used for animal feed and birds.

Oats: Oats are well known as breakfast in almost all the countries. It’s contains a lot of fiber property. They are vital for all ages and also used as livestock feed.

Rye: This is a key cereal grown in cold climates. They are used for making breads, beer, whiskeys, vodka and also used animal feeds.

Buckwheat: This cereal is mainly used in pancakes, noodles and porridge. It is used as wheat cereal, but its characteristics are not the same as wheat. This cereal is also very high in protein and amino acids.
